\section{Verdicts on H1--H3}
\label{sec:Verdicts}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------

\paragraph{H1 (XPBD degrades under high mass ratios) ---
\emph{TODO: confirmed / refuted}.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Tied to \autoref{ssec:MassRatioPendulum} and \autoref{ssec:MassRatioChain}.
    \item Mechanism (if confirmed): dual updates propagate slowly through chains of strongly mismatched masses, because each Gauss--Seidel sweep changes only one constraint's multiplier, and the change couples to the next constraint only through the position update on the shared particle.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{H2 (VBD degrades under high stiffness ratios) ---
\emph{TODO: confirmed / refuted}.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Tied to \autoref{ssec:StiffnessRatioChain} and \autoref{ssec:StiffRatioTest}.
    \item Mechanism (if confirmed): the local Hessian block $\mathbf{H}_{i}$ at a vertex sums contributions from every incident spring, so widely varying stiffnesses explode the block's condition number, slowing the Newton step.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{H3 (VBD's per-iteration convergence is more stiffness-robust)
--- \emph{TODO: confirmed / refuted}.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Tied to \autoref{ssec:ResidualConvergence} and \autoref{ssec:IterIndepStiff}.
    \item Mechanism (if confirmed): VBD uses second-order information about $E$ locally, so increasing $k$ does not change the number of Newton steps to convergence much; XPBD discards this information.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Joint reading.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item If H1, H2, H3 all hold, the framing is consistent: each solver is robust exactly in the regime where its update direction sees the right information, and brittle where the other side of the dual would.
    \item The practical recommendation in that case is regime-based, not absolute --- itself a useful contribution if the experiments support it.
\end{itemize}

\section{Threats to validity}
\label{sec:ThreatsValidity}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Single-threaded execution}
\label{ssec:ThreatSingleThread}
\begin{itemize}
    \item All measurements run single-threaded on CPU.
    \item VBD is known to parallelise better than XPBD (\autoref{sec:Parallelisation}), so ms/frame results underestimate VBD's likely production advantage.
    \item This affects external validity of any quantitative efficiency claim but does not invalidate qualitative claims about convergence quality or stability envelopes.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Mass-spring discretisation only}
\label{ssec:ThreatMassSpring}
\begin{itemize}
    \item I use distance-spring energies throughout, including for bending; real cloth simulators use FEM-based stretch energies and dihedral bending.
    \item The primal/dual framing carries over (both XPBD and VBD admit arbitrary elastic energies), but the specific stiffness ratios encountered --- and therefore the magnitude of the H2/H3 effects --- may differ.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Gauss--Seidel order dependence}
\label{ssec:ThreatOrder}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Both solvers are order-sensitive (\autoref{sec:GSJacobi}).
    \item I fix a single deterministic order; an adversarial reordering could in principle shift results.
    \item I do not investigate this, and it is a real threat to reproducibility on other implementations.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Mismatched collision models}
\label{ssec:ThreatCollision}
\begin{itemize}
    \item XPBD uses projection-based self-collision; VBD uses penalty-based self-collision (\autoref{sec:SelfCollision}).
    \item Any difference in the self-collision experiments mixes algorithmic and contact-model effects.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Fixed $dt = 1/30$~s}
\label{ssec:ThreatDt}
\begin{itemize}
    \item $dt = 1/30$~s is generous for Unity but stresses both solvers.
    \item A different fixed $dt$, or an adaptive step, could shift the equal-wall-clock frontier.
    \item The stability map in \autoref{ssec:StabilityMap} mitigates this by characterising both solvers over a $dt$ range, but the headline results in \autoref{sec:EffResults} are tied to a single $dt$.
\end{itemize}

%----------------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:Limitations}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Beyond the threats above, three further limitations are worth naming:
\begin{itemize}
    \item I do not implement AVBD or any post-2024 VBD extension.
    \item Self-collision uses a simple model; production simulators often use continuous collision detection (CCD), which both solvers can be augmented with at additional cost.
    \item Friction is not modelled; the experiments are friction-free.
\end{itemize}
