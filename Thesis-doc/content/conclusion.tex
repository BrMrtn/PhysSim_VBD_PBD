\section{Summary}
\label{sec:Summary}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------
This thesis presented a controlled comparison of XPBD and VBD, the two
dominant solvers for real-time deformable simulation. The contribution is
threefold: a shared Unity codebase implementing both solvers under matched
material parameters and compute budget; a Newton ground-truth reference
that lets us report solution error rather than only relative performance;
and an experimental design driven by the primal/dual theory of
\cite{Chen2024}, organised around three falsifiable hypotheses
(H1--H3).

\section{Future work}
\label{sec:FutureWork}
%----------------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Multithreaded and GPU comparison}
\label{ssec:FWParallel}
The most consequential extension is to repeat the experiments on a
multithreaded CPU and on the GPU. Both solvers admit parallelisation via
graph colouring, but the structures differ in granularity
(\autoref{sec:Parallelisation}). The expected outcome is that VBD's
per-vertex blocks colour more cleanly than XPBD's per-constraint blocks,
amplifying its production advantage; quantifying this gap is the natural
next study.

\subsection{Extend research to other dynamic types}
\label{ssec:FWOtherDynamics}
Beside mass-spring systems, in theory, both solver could also handle other chloth representations,
soft bodies and rigid bodies as well. It would be interesting to see how the two solvers perform on these other types of dynamics, and whether the same conclusions hold.

\subsection{FEM constraints}
\label{ssec:FWFEM}
Replacing mass-spring with FEM-based stretch and bending energies would
both lift the realism ceiling and test whether the H1--H3 effects observed
here survive the change of discretisation. The primal/dual framing
predicts they should, but only experiments can confirm.

\subsection{AVBD and post-2024 variants}
\label{ssec:FWAVBD}
Augmented VBD~\cite{Chen2024} reportedly handles wide stiffness ratios
substantially better than canonical VBD. Re-running the H2 and stiffness-
ratio experiments with AVBD would clarify whether the gap we observe is
intrinsic to the primal approach or to the canonical 2024 implementation
specifically.
